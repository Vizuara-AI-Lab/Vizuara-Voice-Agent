## 1. Company Overview

**Vizuara AI Labs** is an AI education and research company founded in **2023** and headquartered in **Pune, India**. Vizuara AI Labs has trained over **50,000+ students globally** through its live and self-paced AI/ML courses, research bootcamps, and specialized programs.

Vizuara AI Labs was founded by three alumni of **IIT Madras**, who hold PhDs from **MIT** and **Purdue University**. The company offers programs for college students, industry professionals, school students (K-12), and teachers.

**Mission:** To democratize world-class AI education by teaching from first principles — combining fundamentals, practicals, and a research mindset (the **FPR approach**).

**Key Facts:**
- **Founded:** 2023
- **Headquarters:** Bengaluru, India
- **Students Trained:** 50,000+
- **Academic Backing:** MIT, Purdue University, IIT Madras
- **Website:** [vizuara.ai](https://vizuara.ai)

### 3.2 Vizuara AI Pods — Short-Format AI Mastery Platform

**Video:** [YouTube — Vizuara AI Pods Announcement](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7qCvLUmS0a4)

**Vizuara AI Pods** is a short-format learning platform designed for busy professionals and students who want to go from zero to mastery in any AI concept in **1–2 weeks** (instead of months of long-format content).

**The Problem It Solves:** "Every month you are seeing something new coming up like context engineering, agentic AI, etc. and you do not have the time to read research papers, you do not have the time to do projects on your own." Many learners find 90–100 minute lectures too long and suffer from **FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)** as the AI field evolves rapidly.

**Three Levels of Mastery (FPR Technique):**
1. **Fundamentals** — Learn the basics of the subject from a foundational level (e.g., what is context engineering, how does it work, what are its components)
2. **Practicals** — Implement hands-on Google Colab notebooks specifically designed for the topic — whatever you learned in theory, you implement in practice
3. **Research** — Solve a practical case study based on a **real-life dataset** (not a toy dataset), giving complete confidence to implement end-to-end projects from scratch. The case study is a novel problem statement that can be added to your resume.

**Key Features:**
- Content updated **every 2 weeks** — you never feel you're losing out
- Addresses AI FOMO with a single platform for all emerging concepts
- Placement-ready — case study research can be added to resumes
- Topics include: Context engineering, agentic AI, video generation models, and more
- Takes 1–2 weeks maximum per concept from foundation to mastery

**Target Audience:** Students who lack patience for long-form content, busy industry professionals wanting to stay current, anyone who wants quick mastery of specific AI concepts.

## 14. Learning Philosophy — FPR Approach

Vizuara AI Labs follows the **FPR (Fundamentals, Practicals, Research) approach** to AI education:

1. **Fundamentals** — Deep understanding of mathematical and theoretical foundations (not just API calls)
2. **Practicals** — Hands-on implementation with real code, real projects, and industry-grade tools
3. **Research Mindset** — Encouraging original thinking, paper reading, and publication at conferences

This philosophy is reflected across all Vizuara programs — from the 10-course bundle to research bootcamps. Vizuara teaches AI "from scratch," building models from first principles rather than just using pre-built libraries.

**Q: What is Vizuara AI Labs?**

**Q: Who teaches the courses at Vizuara?**
A: All courses are taught by Vizuara's three co-founders — Dr. Raj Dandekar (MIT PhD), Dr. Rajat Dandekar (Purdue PhD), and Dr. Sreedath Panat (MIT PhD). All three hold B.Tech degrees from IIT Madras.

**Q: What makes Vizuara different from other AI courses?**
A: Vizuara follows the FPR approach — Fundamentals, Practicals, Research. They teach AI from first principles (building models from scratch), not just API usage. All instructors are active researchers with PhDs from MIT and Purdue. They also publish research papers (HULLMI, NanoVLMs) and have authored a book published by Manning.

### Research Programs

**Q: Can I publish a research paper through Vizuara?**
A: Yes. Vizuara offers **five dedicated research bootcamps**: (1) CV Research Bootcamp (₹95,000), (2) RL Research Bootcamp (₹95,000), (3) ML/DL Research Bootcamp, (4) Scientific ML Research Bootcamp (plans from ₹20,000 to ₹1,20,000), and (5) GenAI Professional Research Bootcamp — all designed to guide you from beginner to a published research paper. The Industry Professional tier of the Context Engineering Workshop also includes 2 months of research guidance. Vizuara's mentors have published 20+ papers, with recent acceptances at NeurIPS workshops, ICCV workshops, ACM CIKM (Best Paper Award), MIT URC, MSML (7 papers), and JuliaCon (4 papers).

**Q: How does the research bootcamp process work?**
A: The research bootcamps follow a structured two-phase approach. Phase 1 (6–7 weeks) builds your foundations with self-paced material customized to your research area. Phase 2 (2.5–4 months) is guided research: you start with topic selection, conduct a literature review, formulate a hypothesis, run experiments, iterate on results, write the paper collaboratively on Overleaf, and target appropriate conferences/journals. The mentors identify problem statements that balance **novelty** (impactful enough to publish) with **feasibility** (achievable in a few months). Communication happens through email, Loom (async video), and Overleaf.

**Q: Can I join a research bootcamp as a complete beginner?**
A: Yes. All five research bootcamps (CV, RL, ML/DL, SciML, GenAI) accept complete beginners. Phase 1 covers all necessary foundations. If you already have prior experience, you can discuss skipping Phase 1 with the mentors and jump directly into the research phase. The SciML bootcamp specifically states: "even if you have zero background in ML — no problem, you will confidently transition to ML after taking this bootcamp."

**Q: How much time do I need to commit for a research bootcamp?**
A: Approximately 8–12 hours per week. The mentors understand that some weeks you may be busier with personal or professional commitments — flexibility is built into the program.

**Q: What is Scientific Machine Learning (SciML)?**
A: Scientific Machine Learning combines traditional science (physics, biology, chemistry) with ML. Instead of toy Kaggle projects, you solve real scientific problems — modeling Covid-19 infections, simulating black hole dynamics, predator-prey systems, chemical reactions, and more. The SciML bootcamp uses the **Julia** programming language and covers Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), Neural ODEs, and Universal Differential Equations. It has ~40 research problem statements across biology, physics, mechanical engineering, chemistry, economics, material sciences, and astronomy.

**Q: Which research bootcamp is right for me?**
A: It depends on your area of interest:
- **CV Research Bootcamp** — If you're interested in computer vision (object detection, segmentation, vision transformers, multimodal models)
- **RL Research Bootcamp** — If you're interested in reinforcement learning (reasoning models, robotics, agentic AI)
- **ML/DL Research Bootcamp** — If you want a general ML/DL research foundation (best for career transitioners from any background)
- **SciML Research Bootcamp** — If you're an engineer/scientist who wants to apply ML to physics, biology, chemistry, or other scientific domains (uses Julia)
- **GenAI Professional Research Bootcamp** — If you're focused on LLMs, RAG, AI agents, and production generative AI systems

**Q: What happens if my paper gets rejected from a conference?**
A: Rejection is a normal part of the scientific research process. The mentors will help you target multiple venues. If one conference doesn't work out, they will identify alternative conferences, workshops, or journals that are a good fit for your paper.

**Q: Is the high school research program available for all ages?**
A: The high school research program is designed for students aged 10–17.
